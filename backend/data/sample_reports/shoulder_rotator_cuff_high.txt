MRI SHOULDER - LEFT (WITHOUT CONTRAST)

CLINICAL HISTORY: 61-year-old male, acute shoulder pain and weakness after fall onto outstretched arm.

TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the left shoulder was performed without intravenous contrast.

FINDINGS:
There is a full-thickness tear of the supraspinatus tendon with retraction of the tendon stump to the level of the glenoid, measuring approximately 3 cm. Moderate to severe muscle atrophy of the supraspinatus.
Associated moderate subacromial-subdeltoid bursitis.
Partial-thickness tear of the anterior infraspinatus tendon.
Mild degenerative changes of the acromioclavicular joint.

IMPRESSION:
1. Full-thickness, retracted tear of the supraspinatus tendon with associated muscle atrophy - high-grade injury.
2. Partial-thickness tear of the infraspinatus tendon.
3. Moderate bursitis.
