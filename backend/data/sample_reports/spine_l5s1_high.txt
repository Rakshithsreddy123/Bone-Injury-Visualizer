MRI LUMBAR SPINE (WITHOUT CONTRAST)

CLINICAL HISTORY: 45-year-old male, acute severe low back pain with radiating pain and numbness down the right leg after lifting injury.

TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the lumbar spine was performed without intravenous contrast.

FINDINGS:
At L5-S1, there is a large right paracentral disc herniation with extrusion and caudal migration, causing severe narrowing of the right lateral recess and moderate compression of the traversing right S1 nerve root.
Moderate central canal narrowing at this level.
Mild degenerative disc changes at L4-L5, without significant stenosis.
No bone marrow edema or fracture.

IMPRESSION:
1. Large L5-S1 disc herniation with extrusion, severe right lateral recess narrowing and right S1 nerve root compression - high-grade finding correlating with clinical radiculopathy.
2. Mild degenerative changes at L4-L5.
