MRI LUMBAR SPINE (WITHOUT CONTRAST)

CLINICAL HISTORY: 55-year-old male, longstanding lower back pain, worsening over the past month.

TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the lumbar spine was performed without intravenous contrast.

FINDINGS:
At L3-L4, mild disc desiccation with a small central disc bulge, no significant canal narrowing.
At L4-L5, moderate disc space narrowing with a moderate broad-based bulge and facet hypertrophy, resulting in moderate central canal stenosis.
At L5-S1, mild posterior disc bulge with mild bilateral foraminal narrowing.
Mild loss of lumbar lordosis.
No acute fracture or marrow edema at any level.

IMPRESSION:
1. Multilevel degenerative disc disease, most pronounced at L4-L5 with moderate central canal stenosis.
2. Mild findings at L3-L4 and L5-S1.
