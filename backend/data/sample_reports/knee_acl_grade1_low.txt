MRI KNEE - RIGHT (WITHOUT CONTRAST)

CLINICAL HISTORY: 24-year-old male, twisting injury during football, mild pain and swelling.

TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the right knee was performed without intravenous contrast.

FINDINGS:
The anterior cruciate ligament demonstrates mild increased signal intensity with intact fibers and normal contour, consistent with a low-grade sprain. No discontinuity identified.
The posterior cruciate ligament is intact.
Medial and lateral collateral ligaments are intact.
Medial and lateral menisci demonstrate normal morphology and signal, no tear identified.
No significant joint effusion.
No bone marrow edema.
Articular cartilage is preserved throughout.

IMPRESSION:
1. Grade I (low-grade) sprain of the anterior cruciate ligament. No tear.
2. No meniscal or collateral ligament injury.
