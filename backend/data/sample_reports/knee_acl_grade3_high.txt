MRI KNEE - RIGHT (WITHOUT CONTRAST)

CLINICAL HISTORY: 19-year-old male, skiing accident, unable to bear weight, severe pain and swelling.

TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the right knee was performed without intravenous contrast.

FINDINGS:
The anterior cruciate ligament is completely disrupted with a full-thickness tear at the mid-substance. The torn fibers are retracted and wavy in contour. No intact fibers identified.
Large joint effusion with hemarthrosis.
Severe bone marrow edema involving the lateral femoral condyle and posterolateral tibial plateau.
Associated tear of the medial meniscus, posterior horn, complex type.
Medial collateral ligament shows high-grade sprain with partial fiber disruption.

IMPRESSION:
1. Complete (Grade III) full-thickness tear of the anterior cruciate ligament with retraction.
2. Complex tear of the posterior horn of the medial meniscus.
3. High-grade sprain of the medial collateral ligament.
4. Large joint effusion with hemarthrosis and severe bone contusions, consistent with a high-energy pivot injury.
