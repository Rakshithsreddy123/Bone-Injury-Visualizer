MRI KNEE - LEFT (WITHOUT CONTRAST)

CLINICAL HISTORY: 29-year-old female, basketball injury with pop sensation, moderate pain.

TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the left knee was performed without intravenous contrast.

FINDINGS:
The anterior cruciate ligament shows increased T2 signal with partial disruption of fibers involving approximately 50% of the ligament substance, consistent with a partial tear. Mild laxity noted.
Small joint effusion present.
Mild bone marrow edema in the lateral femoral condyle and posterolateral tibial plateau, consistent with a pivot-shift type bone contusion pattern.
Menisci are intact.
Collateral ligaments intact.

IMPRESSION:
1. Partial (Grade II) tear of the anterior cruciate ligament, involving roughly half of the ligament fibers.
2. Associated bone contusions of the lateral femoral condyle and posterolateral tibial plateau, mild.
3. Small joint effusion.
